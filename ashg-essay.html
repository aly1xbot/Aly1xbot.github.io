ASHG DNA Day Essay

Comment

Yuancheng Luo

For patients, Artificial Intelligence (AI) will be helpful for their genetic testing, and its adoption stands as a pivotal choice in embracing future innovations. Recent developments in AI catalyze prominent changes in biology and medical areas, like helping respond to COVID-19(van der Schaar et al., 2021). Even though some concerns are left unresolved, AI can assist in interpreting the genetic testing result, giving people a more objective perspective on potential health risks.


Currently, genetic testing is widely employed in assessing potential diseases and guiding personalized medicine, with approaches ranging from single variant tests, and gene-based tests, to the Next Generation Sequencing (NGS)(Franceschini et al., 2018). To accelerate the testing and verify their decision, doctors use AI to generate information consent forms and attribute pre- and post-test education to patients to improve their confidence. Under other circumstances, AI can be used for DNA data analysis like identification of variants during testing and analyzing the pathogenicity of the genetic variation(Aradhya et al., 2023).

Benefits and Drawbakcs of AI in genetic Testing

In the most general terms, AI can be involved in the pre-and post-test education of genetic testing to the patients. Genomic Information Assistant (GIA), a SOC-2 certified AI model, was developed to address questions from patients and attribute the pre-test education. GIA has been trained by professional clinicians, resulting in most patients (87%) expressing that GIA is informative in understanding their testing results(Soley et al., 2023). Additionally, GIA is trained to generate genetic research consent forms, clarifying test outcomes, which reported 96% of the patients can get comprehensive information and 86% of patients have a positive experience with this new method(Smith et al., 2023). This application of AI as an educational and interpretation tool fosters a clearer understanding of processes, reducing patients' anxiety when receiving results.

Artificial intelligence (AI)—advanced computer systems that can quickly analyze large amounts of data—is being used in many areas of healthcare, from diagnosing diseases to recommending treatments. Now, experts are also using AI to help interpret genetic testing results, which examine your DNA to understand your risk for certain diseases or guide treatments. If you had to undergo genetic testing, would you want AI to be used to help make sense of your results?

Other than performing educational roles, AI offers profound benefits in the data analysis phase after finishing the gene sequencing. In one study, machine learning (ML) model AlphaMissense, deriving partially from the AlphaFold-derived system, complete prediction on the pathogenicity of missense mutation, classifying 57% as benign and 32% as pathogenic and obtaining accuracy of 90% verified by ClinVar data(Cheng et al., 2023). In addition, AI can process the primary data, make variant identification, and classify. The DRAGEN Illumina model can detect the gene variants in individuals' genome data with high accuracy over 95% and shorten the time to a couple of minutes(Behera et al., 2024). Thus, combining the DRAGEN model with the AlphaMissense together will enable an end-to-end process of variant identification and pathogenicity evaluation.


Despite the undeniable potential of AI in genetic tests, its implementation is not without challenges. Using AI still needs to face the concerns about data transparency and risk with data sharing(Niggli et al., 2022). Also, the lack of empirical evidence for AI analysis will make people determine whether the analysis is proper and if an error occurs, who needs to take that responsibility(Cheng et al., 2023; Chustecki, 2024). Clinicians relying more on automated genetic test education and consent forms will cause potential disputes between patients and clinicians on the treatment for disease, causing unnecessary misunderstanding and unsatisfying of patients when the question goes beyond the training. So further governance and regulations are required to ensure consumers have a nice experience.


For patients, the integration of AI in genetic testing is compelling. Generative AI can analyze the genetic testing results and provide information about patients' diagnoses and advice on the patient's actions. Also, AI's ability to collect large datasets and synthesize the data into multiple pieces of evidence will make patients become more confident and trust the advice more. This is not offered by the traditional genetic test result, the lack of explanation to patients with only complex data and advice demonstrated hard to ensure patients gain full understanding. Worsening by the long time required to obtain the genetic testing result, traditional methods further decrease the accessibility of genetic testing for some patients(Faye et al., 2024). Hence, the results provided will be partial and often uninformative. In contrast, the advice from AI, especially the NLP model, will offer comprehensive insights and guarantee an informed and cooperative patient experience.


Harnessing the AI's computational capabilities in both data analysis and patient education can accelerate the process of normalizing genetic testing as a standard clinical tool. This synergy can enhance the decision-making process of doctors and patients, and make health care more transparent, efficient, and supportive. The interplay between AI and genetic tests has and will unlock more opportunities for people, positioning AI's critical role in future personalized medicine.
