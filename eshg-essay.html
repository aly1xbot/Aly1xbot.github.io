Importance of Genetic Diversity among the disease and health

Comment

Yuancheng Luo

The concept of genetic diversity is aroused from human studying in the disease and public symptom relationship. With more genetic diversity, there is less likelihood the public will get severe diseases like parasites because the lack of generality provides important protection from the pathogen which only works for typical organism mechanisms[1]. Genetic diversity exists in many ways in the natural world, it may derive from the fame shift mutation, different codon or single nucleotide mutation and deletion, and also various expressions in transposon elements. All of them may result in different trait demonstrations and hence the future evolution under pressure[2]. But the potential within it is not widely explored by the human being, with lots of vacancies waiting for amending, people need to focus on diversity investigation and acquiring a deeper understanding of the disease and health that is deeply related to it.


Human genetic diversity is a double-sided sword that gives people both benefits but also offers humans with potential for some non-transmissible diseases like sickle cell anaemia and high susceptibility to chronic heart disease. In Africa, the human genetic change in PCSK9 alters the lipid profile which makes Africans more prone to non-transmissible diseases like chronic heart disease[3]. Another human genetic sequence, like APOEε4, will increase people's susceptibility to Alzheimer's disease if experience mutation[4]. Also, other human genetic variances can affect human susceptibility to transmissible diseases like COVID-19. The researchers have discovered that due to the usage of angiotensin-converting enzyme 2 (ACE2) receptor, people with polymorphism of type 2 transmembrane serine proteases (TMPRSS2) are at higher risk of COVID-19 infection[5]. Further from this, a common source of DNA mutation that causes human genetic disease is the polymorphism of intrachromosomal segmental duplication (SD)[6]. Revealed in the first draft sequence of the human genome, the SD is demonstrated to lots of intrachromosomally and intrachromosomally duplication of the genetic sequence, which the sequences that have been frequently duplication were copy associated with female pregnancy (PSG), metabolic process (CYP1, CYP2) and immune response (defensin, KIR2DL)[6]. With all these increases in genetic diversity, the error during duplication or misplacing of the promoter or regulatory sequence will all lead to a potential for severe genetic disease.

Genetic Diversity with Health and Disease

S

"Genetics is the science of diversity, and diversity is what makes life so beautiful," --Barbara McClintock


Meanwhile, the genetic diversity of humans is also important for our health as it alters the genetic scheme of human significantly. Human genetic diversity is the result of long-term evolutionary processes including genetic drift, migration pattern change, and bottleneck effect, which result in various demographic patterns across regions[7]. For humans, as hosts, our diversity will significantly influence the successful rate of pathogen-infected with higher diversity, hence, the more possibility of the occurrence of pathogen resistive gene sequence that can act against the pathogen infection. The African population is reported to have many adaptions due to genetic diversity arising from the evolutionary process[3]. Arise from complex migration patterns, the knockdown of the OSBPL10 gene sequence provides Africans with protection against the dengue virus and flavivirus and the PCSK9 gene nonsense mutation will also bring Africans with healthier lipid profiles compared to Europeans [3]. The importance of human genetic diversity is not confined to providing additional resistance to some diseases, further understanding of it will lead to the possibility of developing precision medicine and other genetic-related treatments[8]. Precision medicine is based on the analysis of genetic data that gives personalized treatment suggestions to maximize the effect and minimize the common side effects of traditional trial-and-error treatment. As an example, the observance of high frequency (90%) of KRAS mutation in Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma focused by the researchers that proposed to manufacture a kind of inhibitor to prevent its function as an oncogene that specialized for this disease only[9]. The strong correlation between human health and genetic diversity, alarms people to focus more on the investigation of human genetic health and developing new treatment methods to cope with the diversity.

For many years in the past century, geneticists focussed on isolated populations to discover genetic traits, including lactose intolerance or sickle cell anaemia. Recent advancements in genetic and genomic technologies have enabled the conduction of large-scale studies in human genetics, such as those by the UK Biobank, Estonian Biobank, and FinnGen.
