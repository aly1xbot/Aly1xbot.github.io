Paper Interpretation: Piwi/PRG-1 Argonaute and TGF-b Mediate Transgenerational Learned Pathogenic Avoidance

Comment

Yuancheng Luo

TThis is the third paper interpretation about the transgenerational epigenetic inheritance. This paper is about the reproductive and metabolic dysfunction which passed transgenerationally due to the polycystic ovary syndrome. This paper, unlike the previous two, including the observation and data collection on human which is rarely seen in TEI research field as experiment or observation including human across several generation is commonly considered as costly and inconveniently.


In this paper “Transgenerational transmission of reproductive and metabolic dysfunction in the male progeny of polycystic ovary syndrome(Risal et al., 2023)”, the researchers uses mice as model in replace of human to verify the observation that the son of the polycystic ovary syndrome will increase the chance of getting obese. The researchers also verified that this transgenerational effect is induced by the small RNA expression change. At the same time, the effect is on maternal side rather than paternal side which not similar to the previous two papers.

Research Background


Polycystic ovary syndrome is a disease that causes the infertility of female and often induced other kinds of disease like type 2 diabetes. The disease is common linking with syndrome like overly obese which the researchers also observe in the offspring of the patients. Also recent studies have found that the genetic factors for PCOS will also increase the risk of cardiovascular disease, ß-cell dysfunction. The similar obesity syndrome is also observed across the generation as far as to F3 generation indicated by some researchers.

Experiment Object In this experiment, the researchers want to determine the deep mechanism for the young age obesity of the child of PCOS woman and also identified differentially expressed gene of small RNA through the GWAS associative analysis. Then the researchers will turn to the mice model by using prenatal androgen exposure to mimic the PCOS syndrome and showing that the obesity effect will be passed transgenerationally to F3 generation and which mechanism takes for this transgenerational effect.

Experiment Result

1. The PCOS son will have altered lipid circulation profile compare to the normal child. The data sample indicates the increase cholesterol concentration and circulation of low density lipoprotein(LDL) for the PCOS sons. Also as the mother have higher BMI which indicates obesity, the son of the obesity mother will also have higher BMI compare to the control group child with mothers' BMI lower than 25.


2.The researchers then using the sncRNA analysis of serum of PCOS son identifies miRNA targeted loci risk gene of PCOS. hsa-miR-1299, hsa-miR-122-5p, hsa-miR-199b- 3p, hsa-miR-199a-5p, hsa-miR-1307-3p, hsa-miR-96- 5p, and hsa-miR-548ar-3p are the 7 DEmiRNA that found both in PCOS son and PCOS mother which indicates the potential of these 7 DEmiRNAs as the factor gene for PCOS.


3.Using mice model, the researchers found that PCOS will cause transgenerational reproductive syndrome to the further generation. The lower testis weight is observed in these PCOS mice offspring F1 and F2 generation, even the only slightly difference is observed in the F3 generation, the profound effect of androgenized and obesity is still existed by decreasing the fertility rate and also lowering the testis weight. When the paternal is treated with androgen and the maternal side is also in obesity, the transgenerationally metabolic dysfunction is also observed in the offspring and the syndrome like higher liver triglyceride is also observed in the F3 generation.


Possible Mechanism


The researchers have discovered the role of sperm and oocyte miRNA and piRNA in passing the effect down the generation. The four X-linked miRNAs: mmu-miR-465a-5p, mmu-miR-743b-3p, mmu-miR-470-5p, mmu-miR-871-3p, mmu-miR-741-3p are up-regulated in the F2 generation. In the obese lineage, he biological processes is mainly enriched the utero embryonic processes which also indicates the potential of embryonic exposure to environmental stress such as the maternal obese forcing the embryo to experience genetic expression change and finally leads to the obesity.


Importance and Impact


The study of transgenerational transmission of reproductive and metabolic dysfunction across male offspring of polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) provides strong evidence concerning the impact of maternal health status on offspring beyond the generation immediately affected. No longer a disease of concern only to female reproductive health, PCOS is now shown to have long-term impacts in male offspring in terms of fertility, hormone level, and metabolic profile.


This research emphasizes that PCOS should not be perceived merely as an ovarian dysfunction in females but rather as an illness having systemic and inheritable implications. Evidence of disruption of metabolism and fertility in male offspring provides evidence of epigenetic mechanisms in transmitting responses to hormonal disruption in the mother. This evidence may shift clinic perception, potentially to earlier and family-centered therapy in managing PCOS. Moreover, this research contributes to the expanding body of evidence supporting the influence of prenatal and perinatal conditions as the principal predictors of long-term health. This research also necessitates increased caution and tracking of those children born to persons afflicted with endocrine or metabolic disorders. Lastly, the research provides further depth into the transmission of disease and introduces such timely questions as prevention, diagnosis, and treatment procedures in both sexes and across generations.
